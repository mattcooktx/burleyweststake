  - type: subheading
    text: Background on the Parable of Ten Virgins

  - type: list
    intro: 'In ancient Jewish times, weddings were significant (and symbolic) events:'
    items:
      - text: '**The Betrothal:** The groom would leave his house and travel to the bride''s home to present a dowry and a marriage contract'
      - text: '**A Long Waiting Period** began. During this betrothal period:'
        nested:
          - The bridegroom returned to his Father's home to prepare the marital home
          - The bride prepared herself for the return of her bridegroom
          - The families & friends would prepare for the wedding feast
      - text: '**Bridegroom Returns:** The bridegroom would travel in a festive procession of family and friends to the bride''s house to bring her to his home.'
        nested:
          - The exact time of the bridegroom's arrival was not known in advance
          - This was often done at night, with torches lighting the way
          - His arrival would be announced with a loud shout
          - The bride and her attendants would need to be prepared for his coming
      - text: '**The Wedding Feast:** Once the groom arrived, they would proceed to his home for the Feast.'

  - type: subheading
    text: Lessons

  - type: list
    items:
      - This parable represents the membership of the Church — not the world
      - All ten were invited to the supper
      - All ten knew the bridegroom would return for his bride
      - All ten had been warned during the entire betrothal period
      - They simply procrastinated — failed to prepare and sadly were left out
      - 'They **knew**, but did not **do**. They procrastinated.'
      - They allowed themselves to be distracted.

  - type: quote
    speaker: Elder Henry B. Eyring · 1997
    text: |-
      "A fallacy is to believe that the choice to accept or not accept the counsel of prophets is no more than deciding whether to accept good advice and gain its benefits, or to stay where we are.

      *But the choice not to take prophetic counsel changes the very ground upon which we stand.* It becomes more dangerous. The failure to take prophetic counsel lessens our power to take inspired counsel in the future. The best time to have decided to help Noah build the ark was the first time he asked. Each time he asked after that, each failure to respond would have lessened sensitivity to the Spirit."
